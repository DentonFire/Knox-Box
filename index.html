What is the Knox System?

The Knox Rapid Access System is a secure emergency access program. It allows firefighters and paramedics immediate access into buildings without forced entry damage or delay. Property owners store keys and access cards in high-security Knox Boxes that are keyed to a master key controlled solely by the Denton Fire Department.

Where is a Knox Box Required?

In accordance with International Fire Code Section 506, a key box is required where access to or within a structure is restricted.

Mandatory Locations:
  • All main entrance doors.
  • Exterior fire sprinkler riser room doors.

Note: Knox is the ONLY approved key box system for the City of Denton.

Installation & Locking

1 Mounting Guidelines

  • Mount near the main entry door.
  • Typically on the right side of the door.
  • Height: Between 48" and 60" above the ground.

2 Secure the Keys

Once installed, you must call Fire Prevention to have an inspector come out and lock your master keys/cards inside the box.

Fire Apparatus Access Gates

Gates across fire access roads must meet strict requirements to ensure rapid entry. Building Safety issues permits for these installations.

‚ö° Electrically-Operated Gates

  • Must override all other systems.

  • Power Failure: Must automatically transfer to "fail-safe" mode (push open manually).
